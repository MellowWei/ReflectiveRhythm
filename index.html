01 · Problem

When self-directed adult learners lose access to themselves before they lose productivity.

Most digital tools designed to support adult learners assume the user arrives ready: attentionally available, emotionally coherent, capable of articulating a goal. In reality, the harder moments of self-directed learning happen before coherence — when attention has fragmented, when emotional state is unreadable to the learner themselves, when a transition between modes has stalled. Productivity-framed tools demand exactly the readiness the learner has just lost.

This project asks a methodological question: can an AI-scaffolded reflective interface support learners in regulating attention before the productivity layer is invoked? Not as a clinical intervention, but as a learning-sciences-grounded design that treats subjective state as first-person data and reflection as a temporal pathway rather than a moment of insight.

Adults do not first lose productivity. They first lose access to themselves. Working hypothesis · Reflective Rhythm 2026
02 · Rationale

Why this study now, and what literature gap it addresses.

Adult attention and emotional self-regulation has emerged as a defining condition of the post-pandemic environment. Recent epidemiological data show that 49.9% of US adults aged 18–24 reported anxiety or depression symptoms in 2023 (KFF 2023), with attention fragmentation and burnout consistently named as core lived experiences (Ipsos 2023). Repeated cross-sectional national data show no return to pre-pandemic mental wellbeing levels (Kiviruusu et al. 2024, Lancet Psychiatry). Existing response infrastructures presume a clinical pathway — diagnosis, referral, treatment — but a substantial portion of this population is non-clinical, neurodivergent-affirming, and resistant to symptom-based framings. They need tools that treat attention and affect as regulable state, not as diagnosable condition.

The framework rests on a phenomenological commitment that subjective state texture — qualia — precedes diagnostic categorization. This commitment draws on Husserl's analysis of internal time-consciousness, Merleau-Ponty's embodied perception, and Varela's neurophenomenology, and engages Chalmers' hard problem of consciousness by designing around it rather than attempting to resolve it. A second theoretical commitment is critical: existing self-regulation tools often embed what Foucault termed biopolitical logic — bodily and attentional states rendered as objects of optimization and surveillance. The proposed framework treats AI-mediated feedback as scaffolding in Vygotsky's sense — present to support internalization of self-regulation, not to replace it — and explicitly rejects framing attention and affect as performance metrics.

The specific gap. Despite extensive parallel research in affective computing, functional music, and self-regulated learning, no current line of work integrates four components in a single intervention: (1) adaptive auditory environment responsive to user state in real time, (2) AI-mediated reflective dialogue that scaffolds without commanding, (3) wearable physiological convergence as validation layer, and (4) explicit non-clinical, neurodivergence-affirming framing. Brain.fm's neural phase-locking research (2024, Nature Communications) demonstrates that functional music elevates attention-associated beta activity by 119% — but the intervention is non-adaptive. Self-regulated learning research from Zimmerman through Bodong Chen's work on AI-scaffolded reflection (InkSpire) establishes the value of reflective scaffolding — but is focused on K-12 task contexts and does not address pre-task state regulation in adult populations. Affective computing (Picard, Empatica) has matured the wearable layer but treats state regulation as recognition-and-response rather than as temporal pathway.

Why 2026. Three developments make this the right moment: large language models have reached the reflective fluency required for state-sensitive scaffolding; consumer wearable HRV sensing has reached research-grade reliability at non-clinical cost; and the post-pandemic adult population has produced a population-scale demand for non-clinical, sovereignty-preserving regulation tools.

03 · Research Questions

Three layered research questions with explicit falsifiability.

The research is structured as three layered RQs — a main-effect question, a multi-layer convergence question, and a mechanism question. Each maps to a directional hypothesis with explicit falsifiability conditions.

RQQuestion · Hypothesis · Falsifiability
RQ1 · Main effect Q: Does an adaptive auditory-AI intervention condition produce greater within-session attentional and emotional state recovery than fixed-rhythm, random-rhythm, and silence baseline conditions, in non-clinical adults reporting attention or affective dysregulation?

H1: Condition D (adaptive + AI scaffolding) will produce statistically significant greater pre/post improvement on at least two of three measurement layers (self-report · behavioral attention task · physiological convergence) compared to Conditions A/B/C, with Cohen's d > 0.4.

Falsifiability: if D fails to outperform A/B/C on ≥2 layers, H1 is rejected and the core intervention assumption requires theoretical revision.
RQ2 · Convergence Q: To what extent do subjective self-report state recovery, behavioral attention task performance, and physiological proxies converge directionally within the adaptive condition?

H2: In Condition D, self-report state recovery will show directional convergence with behavioral measures (sustained-attention task) and at least one physiological measure (HRV-RMSSD or EDA tonic level), with within-subject correlation r > 0.3.

Falsifiability: if self-report improves but behavioral and physiological measures show no directional alignment, H2 is rejected — the intervention may operate through perceived rather than physiologically grounded state change.
RQ3 · Moderation Q: How does the AI reflective scaffolding mode (stabilization vs expansion) moderate the relationship between rhythm condition and state recovery?

H3: AI scaffolding mode will moderate Condition D's effect: stabilization scaffolding will produce stronger recovery when pre-session state shows high arousal; expansion scaffolding will produce stronger recovery when pre-session state shows low engagement.

Falsifiability: if AI mode × pre-state interaction is non-significant, H3 is rejected and AI scaffolding design requires theoretical revision.

Each RQ maps to one publication target (see 09 · Output).

04 · Method

Design-Based Research as the operating method.

The project follows a Design-Based Research cycle in the tradition of Brown (1992), Collins (1992), Cobb et al. (2003), and Sandoval (2014). Each iteration produces three intertwined outputs: a refined prototype, an empirical observation about how learners interact with it, and a theoretical refinement of the underlying claim.

The current iteration sits between Prototype and Test. A working software-only prototype exists; an informal N=5 self-and-friend feasibility pilot is in progress to calibrate the protocol before formal IRB submission and recruitment.

The data structure is designed for learning analytics from the start: every session produces a CSV row with pre/post self-report on five subjective axes, behavioral attention measures (sustained-attention reaction time and accuracy), and a free-text reflective note. Each iteration adds a row; the schema does not change.

05 · Pains

Five recurring breakdowns in adult self-directed learning.

The five pain points below were surfaced through informal interviews and self-observation during the Empathize phase. Each one is a moment where the learner's existing toolkit fails — not because the tool is poorly designed, but because it presumes a coherence the learner has just lost.

  1. Fragmented attention in self-directed contexts

    The learner has time and intent, but cannot assemble the attentional state required to begin. Existing focus tools start the timer; they do not help cross the threshold.

  2. Emotional overload that reads as productivity failure

    What looks externally like procrastination is internally an unprocessed affective state. Productivity language re-encodes a regulation problem as a discipline problem.

  3. Mode transitions that stall mid-shift

    The learner can describe both the prior state and the desired state, but cannot enter the transition. The gap is temporal, not cognitive.

  4. Tools that demand coherence before they provide support

    Most reflective journaling, planning, and metacognitive tools require articulate self-report as the entry condition — exactly the capacity the learner has just lost.

  5. Absent metacognitive scaffolding in AI productivity tools

    Generative AI tools optimize for output. They do not offer structured scaffolding for the learner's reflective process around whether the output is the right artifact, or whether the underlying state is conducive to producing it.

06 · Prototype

What is currently built, and what is deferred.

The Reflective Rhythm prototype is a web-based, software-only system. It does not require specialized hardware, custom sensors, or installed clients. A learner opens the interface, completes a short pre-session self-report, runs through a structured reflective interaction with an AI scaffolding layer, and completes a post-session self-report and a brief behavioral attention task.

ComponentDescription
Self-report5-axis subjective state instrument. Focus, emotional tone, body tension, inner noise, task readiness. Items are mappable to validated instruments (PANAS, BRIEF-A, PSS) for downstream convergence analysis.
AI scaffoldingStructured reflective micro-dialogue. Two modes — stabilization and expansion — differentiated by reflective depth and prompt structure. Logged as a discrete condition variable.
Behavioral taskSustained-attention reaction time task. Standard PVT-style paradigm, no novel development required. Provides an objective convergence layer.
Data exportCSV with pre/post deltas, AI mode, condition, behavioral measures, free-text reflective note. Designed for direct import into R, Python, or learning-analytics pipelines.
DeferredPhysiological sensing (HRV via consumer wearable) is optional in Phase 1 and not required for the methodological claim. Lab-grade instrumentation is deferred to a possible Phase 2.

The broader product design case study — including the system architecture, intervention design history, and prototype stack — is documented separately at mellowwei.github.io/BCI-HRP. The work above is the learning-sciences slice of that program.

07 · Pilot

A four-condition design ready to run.

The pilot tests a single question: does the adaptive reflection condition produce stronger within-session state recovery than fixed, random, or baseline conditions, after controlling for time-on-task and novelty?

ConditionWhat it tests
A · BaselineSilence or ambient sound. Establishes the natural recovery trajectory and controls for regression to the mean.
B · Fixed scaffoldSingle, non-adaptive AI prompt structure. Tests whether reflective prompting alone — independent of adaptivity — produces state change relative to baseline.
C · Random scaffoldRandomized prompt structures, not contingent on self-report. Controls for novelty, prompt variety, and expectation.
D · Adaptive scaffoldPrompt structure adapts to self-report and behavioral signals in real-time. The core hypothesis condition.

The pilot uses a within-subject crossover design with counterbalanced ordering. The minimum credible signal is a directional convergence of self-report state recovery and behavioral attention recovery in Condition D that is not present in A, B, or C.

The contribution is not that adaptivity works. It is that we can tell whether adaptivity works, in a context where randomization to a six-month intervention is impossible. Methodological framing
08 · AI Workflow

A role-differentiated, documented AI workflow.

Three AI systems are used in differentiated roles throughout the research process. The workflow is documented as a research artifact, not concealed.

RoleSystem · Function
StructureGPT. System architecture, research logic, prototype scaffolding, executable code. Used to build the structural body of the prototype.
ExpansionGemini. Adversarial stress-testing, claim audit, pitch development. Used to surface vulnerabilities before external review.
MirrorClaude. Language precision, ethical framing, phenomenological articulation. Used to reflect structural unfolding without independently generating content.

Authorship. Wei Jueran is the sole originator of the theoretical framework, research questions, intervention design, and final decisions. The AI workflow operates as a structured thinking tool, analogous to working with role-differentiated collaborators, and is fully documented within the system. The workflow does not replace original thinking; it structures and stress-tests it.

09 · Output

Three target venues for the work.

The research program is structured to support three publication targets, one per research question. Each paper stands alone; the three together describe a connected research program.

Maps toVenue · Contribution
RQ1 · EmpiricalComputers & Education. The main-effect result from the four-condition pilot. Reports pre/post state change across self-report, behavioral, and physiological layers in Conditions A/B/C/D. Key novelty: first study to test adaptive vs non-adaptive auditory-AI rhythm using a non-diagnostic outcome framework with multi-layer measurement convergence.
RQ2 · MethodsLearning Analytics & Knowledge (LAK). The multi-layer convergence framework as methods contribution: three-layer (self-report · behavioral · physiological) directional alignment as evidence-of-effect in non-randomizable intervention research. A session-level CSV schema and analysis pipeline generalizable beyond this prototype.
RQ3 · TheoryJournal of the Learning Sciences. The AI scaffolding × state interaction result. Reframes "AI reflection" from a single design choice into a state-contingent design space — stabilization scaffolding for high-arousal pre-state, expansion scaffolding for low-engagement pre-state.
